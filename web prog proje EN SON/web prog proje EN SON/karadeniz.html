Black Sea Collard Wraps (Karalahana Sarma)

Collard greens (black cabbage) wrapped with a tasty rice filling, a very typical Black Sea dish.

Traditional Collard Wraps

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch collard greens (black cabbage leaves)
  • 1.5 cups rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp butter or ghee
  • 2–3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • Salt
  • Hot water or broth

Instructions

  1. Wash the collard leaves, remove thick stems and blanch them in boiling water until soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté the chopped onion in butter and olive oil until soft.
  3. Add tomato paste and cook for 1–2 minutes.
  4. Add rice, salt, black pepper and red pepper flakes. Stir for a few minutes.
  5. Add a little hot water and cook the rice halfway, then let the filling cool slightly.
  6. Place a small amount of filling on each leaf and roll tightly.
  7. Place the rolls in a pot, close together in layers.
  8. Add hot water just to cover, drizzle a bit more oil on top and cook on low heat until the rice and leaves are fully soft.
  9. Serve warm, traditionally with yogurt.

Vegan Collard Wraps

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch collard greens
  • 1.5 cups rice or bulgur
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp dried mint (optional)
  • Salt
  • Hot water

Instructions

  1. Blanch the collard leaves in boiling water and drain.
  2. Sauté onion in olive oil until soft.
  3. Add tomato paste, rice (or bulgur), salt and spices. Stir for a few minutes.
  4. Add a little hot water and cook the rice halfway, then let the filling cool.
  5. Roll the filling inside the leaves.
  6. Place them tightly in a pot, pour hot water just to cover and drizzle extra olive oil.
  7. Cook on low heat until leaves and rice are soft.
  8. Serve warm or at room temperature with lemon wedges.

Black Sea Corn Bread (Mısır Ekmeği)

Dense and rustic corn bread, often served with cheese, tea or soup in the Black Sea region.

Traditional Corn Bread

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cornmeal
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup milk or water
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil or melted butter
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease a baking tray.
  2. Mix yogurt, egg, milk and oil in a bowl.
  3. Add cornmeal, salt and baking powder. Stir until combined.
  4. The batter should be thick but pourable. Add a bit more liquid if too thick.
  5. Pour into the tray and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 25–30 minutes until golden on top.
  7. Let it cool slightly and slice. Serve warm.

Vegan Corn Bread

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cornmeal
  • 1.5 cups water or unsweetened plant-based milk
  • 1/3 cup olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C and grease a baking tray.
  2. Whisk water (or plant milk) with oil and salt.
  3. Add cornmeal and baking powder, mix to get a thick batter.
  4. Pour into the tray and smooth the top.
  5. Bake for 25–30 minutes until the top is dry and slightly golden.
  6. Cool a little before slicing and serving.

Hamsili Pilav vs Vegan Rice Bake

A famous Black Sea rice dish usually baked with anchovies around it. Here you also see a vegan mushroom-based version.

Traditional Hamsili Pilav (Rice with Anchovies)

Ingredients

  • 500–700 g fresh anchovies, cleaned
  • 1.5 cups rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 tbsp pine nuts (optional)
  • 2–3 tbsp currants (optional)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil or butter
  • Salt, black pepper
  • Fresh dill and parsley
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Wash the rice and soak in warm salted water for 20–30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Sauté onion in oil until soft.
  3. Add rice, pine nuts and currants. Stir for a few minutes.
  4. Add salt, black pepper and enough hot water to cook the rice almost fully.
  5. When rice is just done, mix in chopped dill and parsley and let it cool slightly.
  6. Oil a round baking dish. Arrange anchovies (skin side down, tails upward) to cover base and sides.
  7. Place the rice mixture in the middle.
  8. Fold remaining anchovies over the top to cover.
  9. Bake at 180°C until anchovies are cooked and lightly browned.
  10. Let rest and gently invert onto a plate to serve.

Vegan Black Sea Style Rice Bake

Ingredients

  • 400 g mushrooms, sliced
  • 1.5 cups rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 tbsp pine nuts (optional)
  • 2–3 tbsp currants (optional)
  • 3–4 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 sheet nori (seaweed), crumbled (optional)
  • Salt, black pepper
  • Fresh dill and parsley

Instructions

  1. Prepare the rice filling: sauté onion, add rice, pine nuts, currants, salt and pepper. Add water and cook until almost done. Mix in herbs and let cool.
  2. Sauté mushrooms in olive oil with soy sauce until they release water and start to brown.
  3. Grease a baking dish. Spread half of the mushrooms on the base and sides.
  4. Sprinkle crumbled nori sheet if using.
  5. Add the rice mixture in the middle.
  6. Cover the top with the remaining mushrooms.
  7. Bake at 180°C for about 20 minutes.
  8. Serve warm, slicing like a pie.

Black Sea Collard Soup (Karalahana Çorbası)

A warming soup made with collard greens, cornmeal and beans – very typical for the Black Sea region.

Traditional Collard Soup (with Meat or Broth)

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch collard greens, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cornmeal
  • 1 potato, diced
  • 1/2 cup boiled beans
  • 100–150 g small meat pieces or bone broth
  • 2–3 tbsp oil or butter
  • Salt, black pepper
  • Water or broth

Instructions

  1. Sauté meat pieces in oil until lightly browned.
  2. Add chopped collard greens and sauté for a few minutes.
  3. Add potato cubes, beans and water or broth.
  4. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat.
  5. Slowly whisk in cornmeal while stirring to avoid lumps.
  6. Season with salt and black pepper.
  7. Cook until greens, potatoes and cornmeal are soft and the soup is slightly thick.
  8. Serve hot.

Vegan Collard Soup

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch collard greens, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cornmeal
  • 1 potato, diced
  • 1 cup boiled beans
  • 3–4 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, black pepper
  • Water or vegetable stock

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a pot and add chopped collard greens. Sauté a few minutes.
  2. Add potato cubes and boiled beans.
  3. Pour in water or vegetable stock.
  4. While stirring, slowly add cornmeal to avoid lumps.
  5. Season with salt and black pepper.
  6. Cook until everything is soft and the soup is slightly creamy.
  7. Serve hot, with lemon wedges if you like.

Fasulye Diblesi (Green Bean Rice Dish)

A simple but very traditional Black Sea dish: green beans cooked together with rice.

Traditional Fasulye Diblesi

Ingredients

  • 500 g green beans, chopped
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 tbsp butter
  • 1–2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, black pepper
  • Hot water

Instructions

  1. Wash the rice and soak in warm water for 15–20 minutes, then drain.
  2. Melt butter and add olive oil in a pot.
  3. Add onion and sauté until soft.
  4. Add green beans and sauté a few minutes.
  5. Add rice, salt and black pepper. Stir for 1–2 minutes.
  6. Add enough hot water to just cover the mixture.
  7. Cover the pot and cook on low heat until beans and rice are soft and the water is absorbed.
  8. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.

Vegan Fasulye Diblesi

Ingredients

  • 500 g green beans, chopped
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, black pepper
  • Hot water

Instructions

  1. Soak and drain the rice.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pot and sauté onion until soft.
  3. Add green beans and cook a few minutes.
  4. Add rice, salt and black pepper. Stir briefly.
  5. Add hot water to just cover the mixture.
  6. Cook on low heat with the lid on until rice and beans are soft and water is absorbed.
  7. Rest for 10 minutes and serve warm.
