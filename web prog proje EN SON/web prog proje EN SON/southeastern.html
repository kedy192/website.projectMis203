Lentil Köfte (Mercimek Köftesi)

A popular and filling appetizer from Southeastern Anatolia, often served with lettuce and lemon.

Traditional Lentil Köfte

Ingredients

  • 1 cup red lentils
  • 1.5 cups fine bulgur
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tbsp tomato + pepper paste
  • 1/2 cup olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili flakes
  • Salt
  • 1–2 spring onions (optional)
  • Parsley, finely chopped
  • Lettuce leaves and lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Wash red lentils and place them in a pot with about 3 cups of water.
  2. Cook until the lentils are very soft and most of the water is absorbed.
  3. Remove from heat and immediately add fine bulgur, mixing well.
  4. Cover the pot and let the mixture rest for about 20 minutes so the bulgur softens.
  5. Meanwhile, sauté the finely chopped onion in olive oil until soft and slightly golden.
  6. Add tomato and pepper paste to the pan and cook for a few minutes.
  7. Add the onion–paste mixture to the lentil–bulgur mixture.
  8. Season with cumin, chili flakes and salt. Mix well with your hands.
  9. Stir in chopped parsley (and spring onions if using).
  10. Shape the mixture into long köfte shapes using your hands and serve on lettuce leaves with lemon wedges.

Vegan Lentil Köfte

Ingredients

  • 1 cup red lentils
  • 1.5 cups fine bulgur
  • 1 large onion
  • 3 tbsp tomato + pepper paste
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Cumin, black pepper, chili flakes
  • Salt
  • Parsley

Instructions

  1. Cook the lentils with 3 cups of water until mushy.
  2. Add bulgur, cover, and let it sit for 20 minutes.
  3. Sauté onion in oil, add the paste.
  4. Mix with the lentil–bulgur mixture and add spices.
  5. Add chopped parsley and shape by hand.

Stuffed Bulgur Shell (İçli Köfte)

İçli köfte is a famous stuffed bulgur dish. Here you can see both the meat version and a walnut-based vegan version.

Traditional İçli Köfte (with Meat, Boiled)

Outer Shell

  • 2 cups fine bulgur
  • 1 cup semolina
  • 1 egg (optional, for binding)
  • 1 tbsp pepper paste
  • Salt, cumin
  • Warm water

Filling

  • 250 g minced meat
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3–4 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp tomato or pepper paste
  • Salt, black pepper, cumin
  • Chopped parsley (optional)

Instructions

  1. For the filling, sauté onions in oil until soft.
  2. Add minced meat and cook until the colour changes.
  3. Add paste, spices and optionally parsley. Let the filling cool completely.
  4. For the shell, soak bulgur and semolina in warm water for about 20 minutes.
  5. Add pepper paste, salt, cumin and knead very well until you get a smooth dough. Add a little more water if needed.
  6. Take a piece of dough, shape it into a ball and then open a hole with your thumb to form a thin shell.
  7. Fill with the cooled meat mixture and close the top carefully, forming an oval köfte.
  8. Boil water with a little salt in a large pot.
  9. Gently drop the stuffed köftes into boiling water and cook for 6–7 minutes, until they rise to the surface.
  10. Serve hot, optionally with lemon and a simple salad.

Vegan İçli Köfte (Stuffed Bulgur Shell – Boiled)

Outer Shell

  • 2 cups fine bulgur
  • 1 cup semolina
  • 1 tbsp pepper paste
  • Salt, cumin
  • Warm water

Filling (Vegan)

  • 2 large onions
  • 1 cup walnuts
  • 1 tbsp paste
  • Salt, cumin, black pepper

Instructions

  1. Sauté onions, add walnuts, paste, and spices.
  2. Soak bulgur + semolina in hot water for 20 minutes.
  3. Add paste and spices, knead well.
  4. Form a shell, fill with walnut mixture.
  5. Boil for 6–7 minutes.

Ali Nazik

Ali Nazik is a rich eggplant and yogurt-based dish, usually topped with minced meat. The vegan version replaces meat and yogurt with chickpeas and tahini.

Traditional Ali Nazik (with Minced Meat)

Base

  • 2–3 roasted eggplants (peeled)
  • 1–2 cups strained yogurt
  • 1–2 cloves garlic, mashed
  • Salt

Meat Topping

  • 250 g minced meat
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1–2 tbsp tomato or pepper paste
  • 2–3 tbsp butter or oil
  • Salt, black pepper, chili flakes

Instructions

  1. Mash the roasted eggplants.
  2. Mix them with yogurt, garlic and salt to form a creamy base.
  3. Spread this eggplant–yogurt mixture on a serving plate.
  4. For the topping, sauté onion in oil or butter.
  5. Add minced meat and cook well.
  6. Add paste and spices, cook until you get a thick sauce.
  7. Place the hot meat mixture on top of the eggplant base and serve.

Vegan Ali Nazik (with Chickpeas)

Ingredients

  • 2 roasted eggplants
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Salt
  • Sauce: tomato + pepper paste, olive oil, chili flakes

Instructions

  1. Mash roasted eggplant with tahini, garlic, and salt.
  2. Add chickpeas on top.
  3. Pour hot salça (paste) sauce over it.

Şiveydiz

Şiveydiz is a local dish made with fresh garlic stalks. Traditionally it includes meat and yogurt, but it can also be prepared fully vegan.

Traditional Şiveydiz (with Meat and Yogurt)

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch fresh garlic stalks
  • 200 g small meat cubes (lamb or beef)
  • 1 cup chickpeas (pre-boiled)
  • 2–3 tbsp oil or butter
  • 1–2 tbsp flour
  • 2 cups yogurt
  • Salt
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Boil chickpeas until soft if they are not already cooked.
  2. Cut garlic stalks into pieces.
  3. Sauté meat in oil or butter until it changes colour.
  4. Add garlic stalks and sauté lightly.
  5. Add water and cook until the meat and garlic are soft.
  6. Add chickpeas.
  7. In a bowl, whisk yogurt with flour and a little water.
  8. Temper the yogurt mixture with some hot broth from the pot, then slowly pour it into the dish while stirring.
  9. Cook on low heat for a few minutes, without boiling too hard, until it thickens.
  10. Season with salt and serve warm.

Vegan Şiveydiz

(Traditionally contains meat and yogurt; this is the vegan version.)

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch fresh garlic stalks
  • 1 cup chickpeas
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Boil chickpeas.
  2. Sauté chopped fresh garlic in olive oil.
  3. Add chickpeas.
  4. Whisk flour with water and pour in to thicken.
  5. Add lemon and salt.
