Erzurum Ketesi – Erzurum

A layered, savory pastry from Erzurum, usually served for breakfast or tea time, filled with roasted flour and butter.

Traditional Erzurum Ketesi

Ingredients

  • Dough: 4 cups flour, 1 cup lukewarm milk, 1/2 cup water (as needed), 1/3 cup oil or melted butter, 1 packet instant yeast (7 g), 1 tsp sugar, 1 tsp salt
  • Filling: 1.5 cups flour, 4–5 tbsp butter, pinch of salt (optional: crumbled white cheese)
  • 1 egg yolk for brushing

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix lukewarm milk, sugar and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until slightly foamy.
  2. Add flour, salt, oil or melted butter and enough water to make a soft dough. Knead for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rise in a warm place for 45–60 minutes, or until doubled in size.
  4. Meanwhile, prepare the filling. Melt the butter in a pan, add the flour and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the flour turns light golden and smells nutty. Add a pinch of salt and let it cool. Mix in cheese if you are using it.
  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into equal balls (about 10–12 pieces).
  6. On a lightly floured surface, roll each ball into a thin circle or rectangle with a rolling pin.
  7. Spread a thin layer of the roasted flour filling over the whole surface of the dough, leaving a small border at the edges.
  8. Fold the sides of the dough toward the center to form a square or envelope-shaped packet, trapping the filling inside. Pinch the seams to seal.
  9. Place each pastry on a baking tray lined with parchment paper. Cover and let them rest for another 15–20 minutes.
  10. Brush the tops with egg yolk. If you like, lightly score patterns on top with a fork.
  11. Bake in a preheated 180°C (350°F) oven for 20–25 minutes, or until the ketes are puffed and deep golden brown.
  12. Serve warm or at room temperature with tea.

Vegan Erzurum Ketesi

Ingredients

  • Dough: 4 cups flour, 1.5 cups plant-based milk or water, 1/3 cup vegetable oil or melted vegan margarine, 1 packet instant yeast (7 g), 1 tsp sugar, 1 tsp salt
  • Filling: 1.5 cups flour, 4–5 tbsp vegan margarine, pinch of salt (optional: spiced crumbled tofu or vegan cheese)
  • 2–3 tbsp plant-based milk mixed with 1 tsp molasses or a pinch of turmeric for brushing

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine lukewarm plant milk (or water) with sugar and yeast. Let stand for a few minutes until the yeast is activated.
  2. Add flour, salt and oil or melted vegan margarine. Mix to form a soft dough and knead until smooth.
  3. Cover and let the dough rise in a warm place for about 45 minutes.
  4. For the filling, melt vegan margarine in a pan, add flour and roast, stirring constantly, until lightly golden and fragrant. Season with salt and let cool. Stir in tofu or vegan cheese if using.
  5. Divide the risen dough into balls and roll each ball into a thin sheet.
  6. Spread a thin layer of the roasted flour filling over each sheet, then fold the sides inward to form a square or packet.
  7. Place the pastries on a baking tray lined with parchment paper, cover and rest for 15–20 minutes.
  8. Brush the tops with the plant-milk and molasses mixture to give a golden color.
  9. Bake in a preheated 180°C (350°F) oven for 20–25 minutes, until puffed and golden.
  10. Allow to cool slightly and serve warm or at room temperature.

Haşıl (Hasıda) – Kars / Erzurum

A simple and comforting bulgur or wheat dish from Kars and Erzurum, traditionally served with melted butter and garlicky yogurt in winter.

Traditional Haşıl

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups bulgur or coarsely ground wheat
  • 4 cups water (or as needed)
  • 1–2 tsp salt
  • 4–5 tbsp melted butter
  • 1–2 cups plain yogurt
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, crushed

Instructions

  1. Rinse the bulgur or wheat under cold water and drain.
  2. Place it in a pot with water and salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  3. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the grains are very soft and most of the liquid is absorbed. Add a little extra water if needed to keep a thick, porridge-like consistency.
  4. When the wheat is fully cooked, remove from the heat and let it rest for a few minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, melt the butter in a small pan until hot and fragrant.
  6. In a bowl, mix yogurt with crushed garlic and a pinch of salt.
  7. Transfer the cooked wheat to a serving plate and shape it into a mound. Make a well in the center with the back of a spoon.
  8. Pour the hot melted butter into the well and over the Haşıl.
  9. Serve immediately, with garlicky yogurt either on top or on the side.

Vegan Haşıl

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups bulgur or coarsely ground wheat
  • 4 cups water
  • 1–2 tsp salt
  • 4–5 tbsp olive oil or melted vegan margarine
  • 1–2 cups plain, unsweetened soy or cashew yogurt
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, crushed

Instructions

  1. Cook the bulgur or wheat with water and salt until very soft and creamy, as in the traditional version.
  2. In a small pan, gently heat olive oil or vegan margarine. You can add a pinch of red pepper flakes if you wish.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix plant-based yogurt with crushed garlic and a little salt.
  4. Transfer the cooked wheat to a serving dish, shape it into a mound and create a small well in the center.
  5. Pour the warm oil or vegan margarine into the well and over the Haşıl.
  6. Serve hot with garlicky vegan yogurt on top or on the side.

Ayran Aşı Çorbası (Ayran Soup) – Kars / Erzurum

A refreshing soup made with boiled wheat, chickpeas and yogurt, served cold or slightly warm.

Traditional Ayran Aşı Çorbası

Ingredients

  • 1 cup boiled coarsely ground wheat (or barley)
  • 1 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 2 cups plain yogurt or buttermilk
  • 1–2 cups water (to thin the yogurt)
  • 1–2 tsp dried mint or a handful of fresh mint, chopped
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Place the cooked and cooled wheat and chickpeas in a large mixing bowl.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the yogurt with water until smooth and slightly thinner than drinkable ayran.
  3. Season the yogurt mixture with salt and adjust the thickness by adding more water if needed.
  4. Slowly pour the ayran over the wheat and chickpeas, stirring gently to combine everything.
  5. Add dried or fresh mint and mix again.
  6. Chill in the refrigerator if serving cold, or warm gently over very low heat if you prefer it slightly warm (do not boil).
  7. Serve in bowls, garnished with extra mint.

Vegan Ayran Aşı Çorbası

Ingredients

  • 1 cup boiled coarsely ground wheat
  • 1 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 2 cups plain, unsweetened cashew, soy or coconut yogurt
  • 1–2 cups water
  • 1–2 tsp dried mint or fresh mint
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Combine the cooked wheat and chickpeas in a large bowl.
  2. In another bowl, whisk plant-based yogurt with water until smooth and drinkable, like ayran.
  3. Season with salt and adjust the consistency with more water if needed.
  4. Pour the vegan ayran over the wheat and chickpeas, stirring gently.
  5. Add dried or fresh mint and mix well.
  6. Chill before serving for a cold soup, or warm gently without boiling if you prefer.
  7. Serve with extra mint on top.

Hıngel (Kars Dumplings) – Kars / Ardahan

Large dumplings filled with mashed potato and onion, served with garlicky yogurt and a chili butter sauce.

Traditional Hıngel

Ingredients

  • Dough: 3 cups flour, 1 egg, about 1–1.25 cups water, 1 tsp salt
  • Filling: 3–4 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed), 1 onion (finely chopped), 2–3 tbsp oil or butter, salt, black pepper
  • Topping: 2 cups plain yogurt, 2–3 cloves garlic (crushed), 3–4 tbsp butter, 1–2 tsp chili flakes

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix flour and salt. Add the egg and gradually add water, mixing until a firm dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 5–7 minutes until smooth. Cover and let it rest for 20–30 minutes.
  3. For the filling, sauté the chopped onion in oil or butter until soft and slightly golden. Add to the mashed potatoes, season with salt and pepper, and mix well. Let it cool.
  4. Divide the rested dough into 2–3 pieces and roll each piece into a thin sheet with a rolling pin.
  5. Cut the dough into large squares (around 6–7 cm). Place a spoonful of the potato filling in the center of each square.
  6. Fold each square into a triangle or bring the corners together to form a dumpling, pressing the edges firmly to seal.
  7. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the dumplings in batches and cook until they float to the surface and the dough is tender.
  8. Meanwhile, mix yogurt with crushed garlic and a pinch of salt in a bowl.
  9. In a small pan, melt the butter, add chili flakes and heat for a few seconds until fragrant.
  10. Drain the cooked dumplings and transfer them to a serving plate. Spoon garlicky yogurt on top and drizzle with the hot chili butter. Serve immediately.

Vegan Hıngel (Potato Dumplings)

Ingredients

  • Dough: 3 cups flour, about 1–1.25 cups water (you can use potato boiling water), 1 tsp salt
  • Filling: 3–4 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed), 1 onion (finely chopped), 2–3 tbsp olive oil or vegan margarine, salt, black pepper
  • Topping: 2 cups plain, unsweetened soy or cashew yogurt, 2–3 cloves garlic (crushed), 3–4 tbsp olive oil or vegan margarine, 1–2 tsp chili flakes

Instructions

  1. Combine flour and salt in a bowl. Gradually add water until a firm dough forms without using egg.
  2. Knead until smooth, then cover and rest the dough for 20–30 minutes.
  3. For the filling, sauté the chopped onion in olive oil or vegan margarine until soft. Mix with the mashed potatoes and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roll out the rested dough into thin sheets and cut into large squares.
  5. Place a spoonful of potato filling in the center of each square and fold into dumplings, sealing the edges well.
  6. Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the dumplings in batches until they float and the dough is cooked through.
  7. In a bowl, mix plant-based yogurt with crushed garlic and a little salt.
  8. Heat olive oil or vegan margarine in a small pan, add chili flakes and let them sizzle briefly.
  9. Drain the dumplings, place them on a serving plate, spoon the garlicky vegan yogurt on top and drizzle with the hot chili oil.
  10. Serve immediately while warm.

Keledoş – Van / Muş

A hearty stew made with green lentils, wheat and local herbs, traditionally soured with kurut (dried yogurt) and topped with caramelized onions.

Traditional Keledoş

Ingredients

  • 1 cup green lentils
  • 1 cup coarsely ground wheat or cracked wheat
  • 200–300 g boiled meat (optional)
  • Local herbs (Işkın, Soryaz) or other leafy greens, chopped
  • 2–3 cups yogurt or reconstituted kurut (dried yogurt)
  • 1–2 cups water (to thin the yogurt)
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 3–4 tbsp butter
  • Salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. Rinse the lentils and wheat separately under cold water.
  2. In a large pot, cook the wheat with enough water until it begins to soften.
  3. Add the lentils to the pot and continue cooking until both lentils and wheat are tender, adding more hot water as needed.
  4. If using meat, add the boiled meat pieces to the pot and simmer together for a few minutes.
  5. Stir in the chopped local herbs or greens and cook until they wilt and blend into the stew.
  6. In a separate bowl, whisk yogurt or kurut with water until smooth and pourable.
  7. Take a ladle of the hot cooking liquid from the pot and slowly add it to the yogurt mixture while whisking to temper it.
  8. Slowly pour the tempered yogurt back into the pot, stirring constantly over low heat. Do not let it boil hard to avoid curdling. Season with salt and pepper.
  9. In a small pan, sauté the sliced onions in butter over medium heat until they are deeply golden and caramelized.
  10. Pour the onion–butter mixture over the stew just before serving.
  11. Serve Keledoş hot in deep bowls.

Vegan Keledoş

Ingredients

  • 1 cup green lentils
  • 1 cup coarsely ground wheat or cracked wheat
  • 1–1.5 cups boiled chickpeas or chopped mushrooms
  • Local herbs or leafy greens (spinach, chard), chopped
  • 2 cups plain, unsweetened cashew or soy yogurt
  • 1–2 cups water or plant-based milk
  • 1–2 tbsp lemon juice or a splash of vinegar
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 3–4 tbsp olive oil or vegan margarine
  • Salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. Rinse the lentils and wheat. Cook the wheat in a pot with water until it starts to soften.
  2. Add the lentils and continue cooking until both are tender, adding more hot water as needed.
  3. Stir in boiled chickpeas or chopped mushrooms and simmer for a few more minutes.
  4. Add chopped herbs or greens and cook until they are soft.
  5. In a bowl, whisk plant-based yogurt with water or plant milk until smooth. Add lemon juice or a little vinegar for extra sourness.
  6. Temper the yogurt by slowly adding a ladle of the hot cooking liquid while whisking constantly.
  7. Pour the tempered yogurt mixture back into the pot slowly, stirring over low heat. Do not let the stew boil vigorously.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. In a separate pan, sauté sliced onions in olive oil or vegan margarine until golden and slightly caramelized.
  10. Pour the onion topping over the vegan Keledoş before serving.
  11. Serve hot as a filling, protein-rich vegan stew.
