Iskender Kebab – Bursa

A famous kebab from Bursa, made with sliced döner served over toasted pide bread, topped with tomato sauce and melted butter, and served with yogurt.

Traditional Iskender Kebab

Ingredients

  • 300 g thinly sliced beef döner
  • 1 pide bread, cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cut the pide into small cubes and lightly toast them until the edges are crisp.
  2. Warm the beef döner slices in a skillet over medium heat until heated through and slightly browned.
  3. In a small saucepan, mix the tomato sauce with the tomato paste, season with salt and pepper, and heat until it starts to bubble.
  4. Melt the butter in another small pan and keep it warm.
  5. For serving, place the toasted pide cubes on a plate, top with warm döner slices, and spoon the hot tomato sauce over the top.
  6. Drizzle with melted butter just before serving.
  7. Serve immediately with a generous spoonful of plain yogurt on the side.

Vegan Iskender-Style Plate

Ingredients

  • 300 g mushrooms, thinly sliced (or marinated tofu / plant-based döner)
  • 1 pide bread or flatbread, cut into cubes (no milk or eggs)
  • 2–3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 2 tbsp tahini mixed with 2–3 tbsp hot water (or extra olive oil)
  • 1 cup unsweetened plant-based yogurt (soy, cashew, almond, or tofu yogurt)

Instructions

  1. Toast the bread cubes in a dry pan or oven until lightly crisp.
  2. In a pan, heat olive oil and sauté the sliced mushrooms (or tofu/vegan döner) over medium-high heat until browned and slightly caramelized. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine tomato sauce and tomato paste, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for a few minutes until slightly thickened.
  4. In a cup, mix tahini with hot water to make a pourable sauce, or use a little extra olive oil instead.
  5. Place toasted bread cubes on a plate, top with the mushroom or tofu slices, and pour warm tomato sauce over them.
  6. Drizzle with the tahini sauce or olive oil.
  7. Serve right away with plant-based yogurt on the side.

Inegöl Köfte – Bursa

Simple and juicy grilled köfte from the town of Inegöl, usually made with only a few ingredients and served with bread and grilled vegetables.

Traditional Inegöl Köfte

Ingredients

  • 500 g ground beef (with some fat for tenderness)
  • 1 onion, grated
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for grilling or pan-searing

Instructions

  1. Place the ground beef, grated onion, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Knead the mixture well for several minutes until it becomes sticky and uniform.
  3. Cover the bowl and let the mixture rest in the refrigerator for 1–2 hours so the flavors develop and the texture improves.
  4. Shape the mixture into long, oval köfte pieces.
  5. Grill or cook them in a lightly oiled pan over medium heat until browned on all sides and cooked through.
  6. Serve hot with bread, grilled peppers, tomatoes, and a simple salad.

Vegan Inegöl-Style Köfte

Ingredients

  • 1 cup grated mushrooms, well-drained
  • 1 cup cooked and mashed kidney beans or green lentils
  • 1 small onion, grated
  • 1/2 cup oats or 3–4 tbsp chickpea flour
  • Salt, black pepper, cumin
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or a little sesame oil

Instructions

  1. Place grated mushrooms, mashed beans or lentils, grated onion, oats or chickpea flour, and spices in a mixing bowl.
  2. Knead the mixture until it holds together and feels slightly sticky. If it is too wet, add a little more oats or chickpea flour.
  3. Let the mixture rest for about 20–30 minutes so the oats or flour can absorb excess moisture.
  4. Shape into long köfte pieces, pressing firmly.
  5. Brush or drizzle with olive or sesame oil and cook in a non-stick pan or on a grill until browned on all sides.
  6. Serve with bread, salad, and grilled vegetables, just like the original version.

Fish Sandwich – Istanbul

A classic street food from Istanbul, usually eaten near the Bosphorus: grilled fish in a soft sandwich bun with lettuce, onion, and plenty of lemon.

Traditional Istanbul Fish Sandwich

Ingredients

  • 2 fish fillets (sea bass or mackerel)
  • 2 sandwich buns
  • 1 onion, sliced into rings
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Lemon slices
  • Salt
  • Olive oil

Instructions

  1. Season the fish fillets with salt and drizzle with olive oil.
  2. Grill the fish on a grill or in a grill pan for 3–4 minutes on each side, until cooked through and lightly charred.
  3. Warm the sandwich buns slightly, then cut them open.
  4. Place lettuce leaves and onion rings inside each bun.
  5. Add the grilled fish fillet, squeeze fresh lemon juice on top, and serve immediately.

Vegan “Fish” Sandwich

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced (or 200 g firm tofu, sliced)
  • 2 sandwich buns
  • 1 onion, sliced into rings
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Lemon slices
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 small piece of nori (seaweed), crumbled
  • 2–3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, crumbled nori, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add sliced mushrooms or tofu and marinate for at least 15–20 minutes.
  3. Grill or pan-fry the marinated mushrooms or tofu until browned and slightly crisp on the edges.
  4. Warm the sandwich buns and fill them with lettuce and onion rings.
  5. Add the grilled mushroom or tofu slices, squeeze lemon juice on top, and serve hot.

Höşmerim Dessert – Balıkesir

A soft and creamy dessert from Balıkesir, made with semolina, milk, cheese, and sugar, served warm or cold.

Traditional Höşmerim Dessert

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 cup semolina
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup fresh unsalted cheese, crumbled
  • 1 small cup sugar

Instructions

  1. Melt the butter in a wide pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the semolina and toast, stirring constantly, until it turns golden and smells nutty.
  3. Slowly add the milk and sugar while stirring to avoid lumps.
  4. Add the crumbled cheese and continue to cook, stirring, until the mixture thickens and becomes creamy.
  5. When it reaches the desired consistency, remove from heat.
  6. Serve warm for a soft texture, or let it cool for a firmer dessert.

Vegan Höşmerim-Style Dessert

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil or olive oil
  • 1 cup semolina
  • 1 cup almond or oat milk
  • 1/2–3/4 cup cashew cream (blended soaked cashews + water) or soft tofu, crumbled
  • 1 small cup sugar
  • Optional: vanilla extract for extra flavor

Instructions

  1. Heat coconut oil or olive oil in a pan.
  2. Add semolina and toast until golden and fragrant.
  3. Stir in plant-based milk and sugar, mixing continuously to prevent lumps.
  4. Add cashew cream or crumbled soft tofu and cook until the mixture thickens and becomes creamy.
  5. Optionally add a little vanilla extract.
  6. Serve warm or after it has cooled, just like the traditional version.

Islama Köfte – Sakarya

Grilled köfte served with bread slices dipped in rich broth and lightly fried, a special street food from the city of Sakarya.

Traditional Islama Köfte

Ingredients

  • For the köfte: 500 g ground beef, 1 onion (grated), 1 slice stale bread (softened with water), salt, pepper, cumin
  • For the bread: Meat broth, sliced bread, chili flakes, olive oil or butter

Instructions

  1. For the köfte, place ground beef, grated onion, softened bread, salt, pepper, and cumin in a bowl.
  2. Knead until everything is well combined and the mixture feels sticky.
  3. Shape into small köfte pieces and set aside.
  4. Heat the meat broth in a pan.
  5. Dip slices of bread briefly into the hot broth, then place them on a tray.
  6. Lightly fry or grill the soaked bread slices in a pan with olive oil or butter until golden on the edges.
  7. Grill the köfte over medium heat until browned and fully cooked.
  8. Serve the grilled köfte together with the broth-soaked bread, sprinkling chili flakes on top if desired.

Vegan Islama Köfte

Ingredients

  • For the köfte: 1 cup cooked green lentils, 1 cup finely chopped mushrooms, 1/2 cup oats or chickpea flour, 1 small onion (grated), salt, pepper, cumin
  • For the bread: Vegetable or mushroom broth, sliced bread, chili flakes, olive oil

Instructions

  1. For the vegan köfte, mash the cooked lentils in a bowl, then add chopped mushrooms, oats or chickpea flour, grated onion, and spices.
  2. Knead until the mixture holds together. If needed, add a little more oats or chickpea flour.
  3. Let the mixture rest for 20–30 minutes, then shape into small köfte pieces.
  4. Heat vegetable or mushroom broth in a pan.
  5. Dip bread slices briefly into the hot broth and then lightly fry them in a pan with olive oil until the edges are golden.
  6. Cook the vegan köfte in a non-stick pan with a little olive oil or on a grill until browned on all sides.
  7. Serve the vegan köfte on top of or next to the broth-soaked bread, sprinkling chili flakes if you like.
