Sıkma – Turkish Flatbread Wrap (Adana - Mersin - Hatay)

A warm flatbread wrap filled with cheese and herbs, popular in Adana, Mersin and Hatay for breakfast or as a street snack.

Traditional Sıkma (Turkish Flatbread)

Ingredients

  • 2–3 cups all-purpose flour
  • About 1 cup lukewarm water (as needed)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 200 g white cheese (feta-style)
  • 1 small bunch parsley, finely chopped
  • Optional: pre-cooked ground meat, lightly seasoned
  • 2–3 tbsp butter or vegetable oil for brushing

Instructions

  1. Place the flour and salt in a large bowl. Gradually add lukewarm water while mixing until a soft but non-sticky dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough for about 5–7 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover and let it rest for 15–20 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, crumble the cheese into a bowl and mix it with the finely chopped parsley. If you are using cooked ground meat, mix a small amount into the filling.
  4. Divide the rested dough into equal balls (about the size of a small orange). Keep them covered so they do not dry out.
  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll each ball into a thin, round flatbread using a rolling pin.
  6. Spread a thin, even layer of the cheese–parsley mixture over one half of the rolled dough, leaving a small border at the edges.
  7. Fold the dough over the filling to form a semicircle or long wrap, gently pressing the edges to seal so the filling does not spill out.
  8. Heat a saj (convex griddle) or a non-stick pan over medium heat. Place one stuffed flatbread onto the hot surface.
  9. Cook for 2–3 minutes on each side until light golden spots appear and the dough is cooked through.
  10. As soon as you remove the flatbread from the pan, brush both sides lightly with melted butter or oil to keep it soft and add flavor.
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling. Cut into pieces and serve warm with tea or fresh vegetables.

Vegan Sıkma (Cheesy Tofu Flatbread Wrap)

Ingredients

  • 2–3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • About 1 cup water
  • 200 g firm tofu, crumbled
  • 1 small bunch parsley, finely chopped
  • 1–2 tbsp olive oil
  • Optional: red pepper flakes or oregano

Instructions

  1. Prepare the dough by mixing flour and salt in a bowl. Add water gradually and knead until you get a smooth, soft dough.
  2. Cover the dough and let it rest for 15–20 minutes while you prepare the filling.
  3. For the filling, place the tofu in a bowl and mash it with a fork until crumbly. Add parsley, a little salt, olive oil, and optional spices such as red pepper flakes.
  4. Divide the dough into equal balls. Roll out each ball into a thin circle on a floured surface.
  5. Spread a thin layer of the tofu mixture over half of the dough, leaving the edges clean.
  6. Fold the dough over the filling to close it, gently pressing the edges so the filling stays inside.
  7. Heat a dry non-stick pan over medium heat. Place the stuffed flatbread into the pan.
  8. Cook each side for 2–3 minutes until brown spots appear and the dough is fully cooked.
  9. Brush lightly with olive oil after removing from the pan to keep it soft and add flavor.
  10. Slice and serve warm as a quick vegan snack or breakfast wrap.

Fellah Köftesi – Bulgur Meatballs in Tomato Sauce (Hatay(Samandağ) - Adana - Mersin)

Small bulgur balls coated in a rich garlic and tomato sauce, a favorite dish from Hatay and the wider Mediterranean region.

Traditional Fellah Köftesi

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups fine bulgur for köfte
  • 3/4–1 cup hot water (for soaking)
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tbsp tomato + pepper paste
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili flakes
  • Salt
  • 2–3 tbsp flour or fine semolina (if needed for binding)
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 1 small bunch parsley, finely chopped
  • Plain yogurt for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Place the fine bulgur in a large bowl. Pour hot water over it just until covered, cover the bowl, and let it sit for about 10 minutes until the bulgur softens.
  2. Once softened, add the egg, 1–2 tbsp of tomato/pepper paste, cumin, chili flakes, and salt.
  3. Knead the mixture with your hands for 8–10 minutes until it becomes sticky and holds together. If the mixture feels too loose, add a little flour or semolina.
  4. Take small pieces from the mixture and roll them into marble-sized balls. Press your fingertip into the center of each ball to form a small indentation.
  5. Bring a large pot of salted water to a gentle boil. Add the bulgur balls carefully and cook until they rise to the surface, about 5–7 minutes.
  6. While the balls are cooking, prepare the sauce. Heat olive oil in a pan, add the chopped garlic, and sauté briefly without burning.
  7. Add the remaining tomato and pepper paste, then stir in the tomato sauce and pomegranate molasses. Simmer for a few minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  8. Drain the cooked bulgur balls and gently transfer them into the pan with the sauce.
  9. Toss everything carefully so that each ball is well coated with the garlic–tomato sauce.
  10. Turn off the heat and mix in the chopped parsley.
  11. Serve warm on a plate or shallow bowl, optionally with a side of plain yogurt.

Vegan Fellah Köftesi

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups fine bulgur
  • 3/4–1 cup hot water
  • 2–3 tbsp mashed potato or 2 tbsp chickpea flour (binder)
  • 3 tbsp tomato + pepper paste
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili flakes
  • Salt
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 1 small bunch parsley, finely chopped
  • Vegan yogurt (soy or cashew) for serving, optional

Instructions

  1. Put the fine bulgur in a bowl and cover with hot water. Cover and let it rest until the water is absorbed and the bulgur is soft.
  2. Add mashed potato or chickpea flour, 1–2 tbsp of the tomato/pepper paste, cumin, chili flakes, and salt.
  3. Knead very well with your hands until the mixture becomes smooth and easily forms balls. Add a little more water or flour if needed.
  4. Shape the mixture into small balls and press a tiny indentation in the center of each one with your finger.
  5. Boil a large pot of salted water. Add the balls gently and cook until they float to the surface, then cook 1–2 minutes more.
  6. Meanwhile, heat olive oil in a pan. Add the garlic and sauté briefly, then stir in the remaining tomato/pepper paste and tomato sauce.
  7. Add pomegranate molasses and a little water if the sauce is too thick. Simmer for a few minutes.
  8. Drain the cooked bulgur balls and add them to the sauce. Toss gently until all the balls are covered with sauce.
  9. Remove from heat and stir in chopped parsley.
  10. Serve warm with vegan yogurt on the side if you like.

Analı Kızlı Çorbası – Mother & Daughter Soup (Adana - Mersin)

A hearty, sour tomato-based stew with two types of bulgur balls (“mothers” and “daughters”) and chickpeas, famous in Adana and Mersin.

Traditional Analı Kızlı Çorbası

Ingredients

  • For the large “mother” meatballs: 300 g ground meat, 1 cup fine bulgur, 2–3 tbsp semolina, salt, pepper
  • For the small “daughter” meatballs: 1 cup fine bulgur, hot water, salt
  • 1 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1.5–2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1–2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 6–7 cups water or meat stock
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp dried mint
  • 1 tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. To make the large meatballs, place the ground meat, bulgur, semolina, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Knead the mixture for several minutes until it becomes very firm and well combined.
  2. Shape the mixture into walnut-sized balls, pressing firmly so they do not break in the soup. Set aside.
  3. For the small meatballs, soak the fine bulgur with hot water and a little salt. After it softens, knead until the mixture is sticky and forms easily.
  4. Roll the bulgur mixture into tiny, chickpea-sized balls and set aside on a tray.
  5. In a large pot, whisk tomato paste into water or meat stock until dissolved. Bring to a simmer and season lightly with salt.
  6. Add the boiled chickpeas to the pot and let the soup base simmer for a few minutes.
  7. Carefully place the large “mother” meatballs into the simmering liquid. Cook gently for 10–12 minutes, trying not to stir too much so they do not fall apart.
  8. Add the small “daughter” meatballs into the pot and continue to simmer until all of the balls are cooked through and floating, about 10 more minutes.
  9. Stir in the pomegranate molasses and adjust the seasoning with more salt or chili flakes if desired.
  10. In a small pan, melt the butter and add dried mint (and chili flakes, if using). Let it sizzle for a few seconds to release the aroma, then remove from heat.
  11. Pour the hot mint butter over the soup and gently stir once.
  12. Serve the soup hot in deep bowls, making sure each bowl has both large and small meatballs and some chickpeas.

Vegan Analı Kızlı Çorbası

Ingredients

  • For the large balls: 1 cup cooked green lentils, 1 cup fine bulgur, 2 tbsp semolina, salt, pepper
  • For the small balls: 1 cup fine bulgur, hot water, salt
  • 1 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1.5–2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1–2 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 6–7 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 3 tbsp olive oil or vegan margarine
  • 1 tbsp dried mint
  • 1 tsp chili flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. For the large vegan balls, mash the cooked lentils in a bowl. Add fine bulgur, semolina, salt, and pepper.
  2. Mix and knead well until the mixture holds together. Shape into walnut-sized balls and set aside.
  3. For the small balls, soak fine bulgur in hot salted water until soft, then knead until sticky and shape into tiny balls.
  4. In a soup pot, mix vegetable broth or water with tomato paste and bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Add the boiled chickpeas and simmer for a few minutes.
  6. Gently add the large lentil–bulgur balls to the pot and cook over low heat for about 10 minutes.
  7. Add the small bulgur balls and continue simmering until all the balls are cooked and float to the surface.
  8. Stir in pomegranate molasses and adjust salt and spices to taste.
  9. In a small pan, heat olive oil or vegan margarine. Add dried mint and chili flakes and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  10. Pour this aromatic oil over the soup and give it a gentle stir.
  11. Serve hot, making sure each bowl includes both sizes of balls and plenty of chickpeas.

Kabak Mücveri – Zucchini Fritters (Antalya)

Light and crispy zucchini fritters from the Mediterranean city of Antalya, often served warm with yogurt on the side.

Traditional Kabak Mücveri

Ingredients

  • 3 medium zucchinis, grated
  • 1 small onion, grated
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 50 g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper
  • Oil for frying
  • Optional: yogurt for serving

Instructions

  1. Wash and grate the zucchinis. Place them in a clean towel and squeeze firmly to remove as much water as possible.
  2. Transfer the grated zucchini into a mixing bowl. Add the grated onion, eggs, flour, crumbled feta, chopped dill, salt and pepper.
  3. Mix everything thoroughly until a thick batter forms. If it is too runny, add a little more flour.
  4. Heat a thin layer of oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  5. Drop spoonfuls of the mixture into the pan and gently flatten each one.
  6. Fry for 2–3 minutes per side until golden brown and crisp at the edges.
  7. Transfer the cooked fritters onto a plate lined with paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  8. Serve warm, optionally with a side of garlic yogurt.

Vegan Kabak Mücveri

Ingredients

  • 3 medium zucchinis, grated and well-squeezed
  • 1 small onion, grated
  • 2 tbsp chickpea flour
  • 2 tbsp water (to make a vegan egg binder)
  • 1/2 cup regular flour
  • Crumbled tofu mixed with a pinch of nutritional yeast and a squeeze of lemon juice (for a feta-like taste)
  • 2 tbsp chopped dill
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Vegan yogurt (soy or almond) for serving, optional

Instructions

  1. Grate the zucchinis and squeeze out as much liquid as possible.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk chickpea flour with water to form a thick vegan egg replacement.
  3. Place grated zucchini and onion in a large bowl. Add the chickpea mixture, flour, tofu mixture, dill, salt and pepper. Stir until well combined.
  4. Heat olive oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  5. Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the pan, flattening them slightly.
  6. Fry each fritter for a few minutes on each side until golden and crisp.
  7. Transfer to a plate lined with paper towels to remove excess oil.
  8. Serve warm with vegan yogurt on the side if desired.

Şekerpare – Syrup-Soaked Semolina Cookies (Antalya / Mersin)

A classic Turkish dessert from the Mediterranean region, made of soft semolina cookies soaked in fragrant lemon syrup.

Traditional Şekerpare

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 125 g butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup semolina
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • For the syrup: 2 cups sugar, 2 cups water, 1 tsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F). Line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, semolina, softened butter, powdered sugar, egg and baking powder.
  3. Knead the mixture until a smooth, soft dough forms.
  4. Take small pieces from the dough and roll them into round shapes. Place them on the baking tray, leaving a little space between each.
  5. Bake for about 20–25 minutes or until the tops are lightly golden.
  6. While the cookies are baking, prepare the syrup. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat slightly and simmer for about 10 minutes. Add lemon juice at the end and remove from heat.
  7. When the Şekerpare come out of the oven hot, slowly pour the warm syrup over them, making sure each piece is covered.
  8. Allow the dessert to rest until the cookies absorb all the syrup and become soft.
  9. Serve at room temperature.

Vegan Şekerpare

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup vegan margarine or coconut oil, softened
  • 1/2 cup semolina
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tbsp ground flaxseed + 6 tbsp water (flax egg)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • For the syrup: 2 cups sugar, 2 cups water, 1 tsp lemon juice (already vegan)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F). Line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, mix ground flaxseed with water and let it sit for 5–10 minutes until it thickens.
  3. In a larger bowl, combine flour, semolina, vegan margarine or coconut oil, powdered sugar, baking powder and the prepared flax “egg”.
  4. Knead until a soft dough forms.
  5. Shape the dough into small balls and place them on the baking tray.
  6. Bake for about 20–25 minutes until lightly golden.
  7. Prepare the syrup by boiling sugar and water together for about 10 minutes, then add lemon juice and remove from heat.
  8. Pour the warm syrup over the hot vegan Şekerpare and let them soak until all the syrup is absorbed.
  9. Serve at room temperature as a fully plant-based version of this classic dessert.
